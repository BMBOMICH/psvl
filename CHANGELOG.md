# Changelog

All notable changes to PSVL are documented in this file.

The format is based on [**Keep a Changelog**](https://keepachangelog.com/en/1.1.0/).
PSVL uses informal two-number license versioning (similar to Apache 2.0,
GPL 3.0, BUSL 1.1, and Elastic 2.0):

- **MAJOR** version (e.g., `2.0`): substantial restructuring or fundamentally
  new obligations
- **MINOR** version (e.g., `1.1`, `1.2`): added clauses, expanded carve-outs,
  significant clarifications, typo fixes, and minor formatting updates

## [1.0] — 2026-05-14

### Initial Release

#### Added — Core Structure

- 9 major sections covering definitions, license grant, permitted uses,
  prohibited uses, contributor terms, legal provisions, warranty disclaimer,
  limitation of liability, and general provisions
- 460+ active sub-clauses
- 31 defined terms in the Definitions section
- Table of contents
- Plain English summary at the top

#### Added — Permitted Uses (Section 3)

- 133 sub-clauses across 12 topical areas covering personal evaluation,
  education and research, security and bug research, performance benchmarking,
  localization and accessibility, community contributions, design and UI,
  integration and interoperability, display and promotion, testing
  environments and devices, infrastructure and diagnostics, and legal /
  humanitarian use

#### Added — Prohibited Uses (Section 4)

- 248 sub-clauses across 15 topical areas covering commercial use bans,
  IP and architecture protections, distribution and deployment restrictions,
  government and institutional limits, AI and machine learning prohibitions,
  security attack bans, data and privacy violations, spoofing and fraud
  prohibitions, automation and botting restrictions, harmful and illegal
  use bans, reverse engineering protections, network attack bans, platform
  integrity rules, exotic and advanced prohibitions, and content and
  branding protections

#### Added — Legal Framework (Sections 5–9)

- Contributor IP assignment with 18+ age requirement and moral-rights
  fallback license
- Governing law, jurisdiction, and binding arbitration with class-action
  waiver (with EU/UK/etc. carve-outs)
- Severability, license termination with cure period, indemnification with
  proportionality carve-out
- Confidentiality with five-year survival, data breach notification within
  48 hours
- Export-control and sanctions compliance referencing GDPR, UK GDPR,
  CCPA/CPRA, LGPD, PIPL, PIPEDA, DPDP Act, POPIA, PDPA, and APPI
- Enterprise insurance requirement with tiered minimums
- Accessibility compliance commitment referencing WCAG 2.2 Level AA,
  EN 301 549, and Section 508
- Force majeure protection
- Disclaimer of warranties including high-risk-use and regulatory-compliance
  carve-outs
- Limitation of liability with carve-outs for fraud, gross negligence,
  willful misconduct, and death or personal injury
